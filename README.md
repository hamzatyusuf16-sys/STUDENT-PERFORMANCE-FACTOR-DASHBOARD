# STUDENT-PERFORMANCE-FACTOR-DASHBOARD
Understanding what drives student academic performance is critical for improving educational outcomes. However, factors such as study habits, lifestyle choices, and socio-economic background are often not analyzed together.  This project aims to identify the key drivers of student success using a data-driven dashboard.

Key KPIs

 Total Students: 6,607
 Avg Study Hours: 20 hrs
 Avg Exam Score: 67
 Avg Sleep Hours: 7 hrs
 Avg Attendance: 80%

 KEY INSIGHTS
 
 Study Habits
Students with very long study hours achieve the highest scores (~75)
Performance steadily declines as study time reduces
Attendance has a strong positive impact:
Excellent attendance → highest scores (~101 scaled view)
Students with very high tutoring support perform best (~69)

 Insight: Consistency in study + attendance is a major success driver

 Lifestyle Factors

Students from high-income backgrounds perform slightly better (~101)
Access to resources:
High access → highest performance (~69)
Low access → lower performance (~66)

 Insight: Resource availability directly influences academic outcomes

Background & Environment

Public school students slightly outperform private in some peer conditions
Positive peer influence leads to better scores across both school types

 Insight: Environment and peer group play a critical role in performance
 
 RECOMMENDATIONS

For Students

Maintain consistent study schedules
Surround yourself with positive peer groups
Utilize available learning resources effectively

 For Schools

Improve access to academic resources
Encourage attendance tracking and interventions
Promote peer mentorship programs
 
 For Parents

Provide supportive learning environments
Encourage balanced study and lifestyle habits
Stay involved in academic progress

CONCLUSION

This dashboard reveals that student performance is influenced by a combination of discipline, environment, and access to resources.
It demonstrates the importance of a holistic approach to education backed by data.
